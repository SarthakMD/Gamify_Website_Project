Step 1: Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

PEMDAS stands for Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division, Addition/Subtraction. Example: Solve 3 + 6 × (5 − 2).

Solution: First, solve inside the parentheses: 5 − 2 = 3. Then, multiply: 6 × 3 = 18. Finally, add: 3 + 18 = 21.

Step 2: Fractions and Decimals

Fractions represent parts of a whole. Example: 1/2 + 1/4 = 3/4.

Decimals are another way to represent fractions. Example: 0.5 + 0.25 = 0.75.

Step 3: Percentages

Percentages are fractions out of 100. Example: 25% of 80 = 20.

Formula: Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100.

Step 1: Solving Linear Equations

Linear equations are equations of the form ax + b = c. Example: Solve 2x + 3 = 7.

Solution: Subtract 3: 2x = 4. Divide by 2: x = 2.

Step 2: Quadratic Equations

Quadratic equations are of the form ax² + bx + c = 0. Example: Solve x² − 5x + 6 = 0.

Solution: Factorize: (x − 2)(x − 3) = 0. Solutions: x = 2, x = 3.

Step 3: Systems of Equations

Systems of equations involve solving multiple equations simultaneously. Example: Solve 2x + y = 5 and x − y = 1.

Solution: Add the equations: 3x = 6 → x = 2. Substitute: y = 1.

Step 1: Pythagorean Theorem

Pythagorean theorem states that in a right triangle, a² + b² = c². Example: Find the hypotenuse if a = 3 and b = 4.

Solution: c² = 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25 → c = 5.

Step 2: Area of a Circle

Area of a circle is given by A = πr². Example: Find the area if r = 7.

Solution: A = π × 7² = 49π.

Step 3: Volume of a Sphere

Volume of a sphere is given by V = (4/3)πr³. Example: Find the volume if r = 3.

Solution: V = (4/3)π × 3³ = 36π.

Step 1: Derivatives

Derivatives measure the rate of change. Example: Find the derivative of f(x) = x².

Solution: f'(x) = 2x.

Step 2: Integrals

Integrals calculate the area under a curve. Example: Find the integral of f(x) = 2x.

Solution: ∫2x dx = x² + C.

Step 3: Limits

Limits describe the behavior of a function as it approaches a value. Example: Find lim(x→2) (x² − 4)/(x − 2).

Solution: Simplify: (x + 2)(x − 2)/(x − 2) = x + 2. So, lim(x→2) x + 2 = 4.
