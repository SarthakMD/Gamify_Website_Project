Learn English Grammar

Step 1: What is a Noun?

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Example: dog, city, happiness.

Step 2: What is a Verb?

A verb is a word that describes an action or state. Example: run, is, think.

Step 3: What is an Adjective?

An adjective is a word that describes a noun. Example: happy, tall, blue.

Step 1: Present Tense

The present tense describes actions happening now. Example: I eat pizza.

Step 2: Past Tense

The past tense describes actions that happened in the past. Example: I ate pizza.

Step 3: Future Tense

The future tense describes actions that will happen. Example: I will eat pizza.

Step 1: Subject

The subject is the person or thing doing the action. Example: The cat sleeps.

Step 2: Verb

The verb is the action or state. Example: The cat sleeps.

Step 3: Object

The object is the person or thing receiving the action. Example: The cat eats fish.

Step 1: Subject-Verb Agreement

The subject and verb must agree in number. Example: He runs (not "He run").

Step 2: Punctuation

Use periods, commas, and question marks correctly. Example: What is your name?

Step 3: Capitalization

Capitalize the first word of a sentence and proper nouns. Example: John lives in New York.
